# REVEAL SCENE - Dialogue Draft v3
Setting: courtyard below Mount Fuji, at the END OF LEVEL 2 (after both
levels' worth of trials are complete — 5 total across Level 1 and 2).
Player has handed the real items to the princes along the way (off-screen
or in a short handoff beat per trial). Princes now present them to
Kaguya as their own.

---

## PART ONE: The Princes' Lie

**PRINCE ISHITSUKURI:**
The Buddha's own begging bowl. I crossed three provinces for it.

**PRINCE KURAMOCHI:**
The jeweled branch of Mount Hōrai, cut by my own hand.

**MINISTER ABE:**
The robe of the fire rat. The flames themselves could not take it from me.

**MINISTER ŌTOMO:**
The dragon's jewel, pried loose in a storm I barely survived.

**LORD ISONOKAMI:**
The swallow's shell. It nearly cost me my life to bring it here.

**KAGUYA:**
*(quiet, studying each item — then looking past them, to the player)*
These are real. Every one of them. That much I don't doubt.

**ŌTOMO:**
Then the trials are complete. Choose.

**KAGUYA:**
I said the treasures were real. I said nothing about the hands that
found them.

*(she turns to the player)*

You were seen at the temple. At the mountain. At the shore. Five times,
in five places these men claim as their own labor.

**ISHITSUKURI:**
*(too quickly)*
A servant. Sent ahead to carry—

**KAGUYA:**
Carrying is not finding. And you were not there to send anyone,
because you were not there at all.

**KURAMOCHI:**
This is beneath a princess's dignity, to take a commoner's word over—

**KAGUYA:**
It isn't his word I'm taking. It's the truth sitting in your hands,
which none of you can explain the first thing about.

**ABE:**
*(the robe, still smoldering slightly from an earlier test)*
Princess, be reasonable—

**KAGUYA:**
I have been endlessly reasonable. Five times over. I'm finished being
reasonable with men who let someone else bleed for their honor.

**ŌTOMO:**
*(drawing himself up, temper snapping)*
Then honor will settle it instead.

*(Ōtomo draws first; the others fall in behind him, cornered)*

**KAGUYA (to player, aside):**
I'm sorry. You did the honest part. Let me help you finish it.

**[BOSS GAUNTLET: Ōtomo (Krampus) → Ishitsukuri → Kuramochi → Abe →
Isonokami]**

---

## PART TWO: NegaKasey's Theft

*(princes defeated, items now gathered and held by Kaguya; player
approaches to finally receive her thanks / the ending beat)*

**KAGUYA:**
You did what five grown men could not. Come — these treasures are
yours to—

*(a shadow falls across the courtyard; the items are yanked from her
hands by an unseen force before she can finish the sentence)*

**NEGAKASEY:**
*(mocking, mirror-image sneer)*
Hers to give? No. Mine to take. You did all the work — I'll take all
the credit. Feels familiar, doesn't it?

**KAGUYA:**
*(shaken, but not cowed)*
Who are you—

**NEGAKASEY:**
*(already fading back into shadow)*
Someone who's been waiting for the two of you to finish the easy part.

*(NegaKasey vanishes with the treasures)*

**KAGUYA:**
*(to player, steadying herself)*
I don't know what that was. But I know where the moon's light still
reaches, and it isn't here.

*(she raises a hand; moonlight gathers and folds the courtyard away)*

**KAGUYA:**
Hold on to me. Wherever he's gone, I'm not letting you chase him alone.

**[KAGUYA TRANSPORTS THE PLAYER TO LEVEL 3 - NegaKasey established as
true final villain]**

---

## Notes for implementation
- Both parts of this scene now happen back-to-back at the END of Level 2
  — the princes' gauntlet and NegaKasey's theft are one continuous
  sequence, not split across levels.
- Part One keeps the emotional beat on the princes lying about *effort*,
  not the items themselves — Kaguya's line "I said the treasures were
  real" front-loads the twist so it doesn't feel like a rug-pull later.
- Ōtomo drawing first still sets up Krampus as the gauntlet's opener.
- NegaKasey's reveal is a hard cut with no foreshadowing dialogue, since
  this is his first appearance — his line now plays as a fresh threat
  rather than referencing history that doesn't exist yet.
- Kaguya transports the player to Level 3 herself via a moonlight
  effect, and explicitly comes along rather than staying behind — gives
  her a reason to be present for whatever NegaKasey-focused content
  Level 3 needs.
