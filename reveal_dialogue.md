# REVEAL SCENE - Dialogue Draft
Setting: courtyard below Mount Fuji, all 5 princes gathered with their
"proof" items. Player has just delivered the final trial item.

---

**PRINCESS KAGUYA:**
Five trials. Five treasures, brought before the mountain's watch.
Set them together, and let me see what devotion looks like.

*(princes present their items; player character stands among them)*

**PRINCE ISHITSUKURI:**
The Buddha's own begging bowl, radiant beyond any temple's altar.

**KAGUYA:**
*(examining it)*
Radiant... and yet it casts no light at all. A potter's kiln, not a
prayer hall, made this.

**ISHITSUKURI:**
*(stammering)*
The temple was— very far. The roads were dangerous. Surely intent—

**KAGUYA:**
Intent does not survive a lie so plainly told.

---

**PRINCE KURAMOCHI:**
From Mount Hōrai itself, a branch heavy with jewels no smith could shape.

**KAGUYA:**
*(turning the branch over)*
A smith shaped it exactly this well. I know craftsmen who would
recognize their own hand in this, if you let them near it.

**KURAMOCHI:**
You wound me. I sailed—

**KAGUYA:**
You commissioned. There is a difference, and you know it.

---

**MINISTER ABE:**
The robe of the fire rat, untouched by flame.

*(a torch is brought near; the robe catches, princes gasp)*

**KAGUYA:**
Untouched, you said.

**ABE:**
*(backing away)*
It was — the merchant assured me — I was deceived as much as—

**KAGUYA:**
Then we are both owed an apology. Only one of us will get it.

---

**MINISTER ŌTOMO:**
*(loudest, most confident)*
The dragon's jewel. I fought for this. Ask anyone who sailed with me.

**KAGUYA:**
I did. None of them remember a storm at all, only your poems about one,
written well before you left port.

**ŌTOMO:**
*(temper rising)*
You dare question a minister's word—

**KAGUYA:**
I dare question a fake stone. The rest follows on its own.

---

**LORD ISONOKAMI:**
*(quietly, already deflated)*
I fell reaching for the shell. I broke half my body for it. Doesn't
that count for something?

**KAGUYA:**
*(softer, genuinely)*
It counts for the only honest injury in this courtyard. But the shell
in your hand is a fisherman's trade good, not a swallow's own.
Courage isn't the same as truth, my lord. I wish it were.

---

**KAGUYA (to all):**
Five treasures, five stories, and not one that survives being looked at
twice. You did not come to win a heart. You came to win an argument,
and dressed it as devotion.

**ŌTOMO:**
Then let the argument be settled another way.

*(Ōtomo draws first; the other four princes fall in behind him)*

**KAGUYA:**
*(to player, quiet aside)*
This part, at least, will be honest.

**[BOSS GAUNTLET BEGINS - Ōtomo (Krampus) → Ishitsukuri → Kuramochi →
Abe → Isonokami, or whichever order plays best]**

## Notes for implementation
- Ōtomo drawing first/being angriest fits reusing the Krampus
  charge-dash-slam moveset — he reads as the aggressive opener.
- Isonokami's exchange is written softer since the myth already has him
  suffer for his attempt; could be the emotional low point right before
  the gauntlet turns serious, or held for last as the "saddest" fight.
- Kaguya's lines stay dry/pointed rather than cruel — she's debunking,
  not gloating, which keeps her sympathetic through the boss rush.
